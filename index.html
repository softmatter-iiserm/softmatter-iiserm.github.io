About Us

We are a set of theoretical, computational, and experimental research groups at IISER Mohali investigating emergent behaviour in complex soft and biological systems, spanning active and soft matter, mechanobiology and cell mechanics, active nematics, biophysical modelling, and transport in non-equilibrium environments.

We combine physics-based theory and large-scale simulations to uncover the principles that govern motion, shape, and organisation in systems far from equilibrium. We also employ a wide range of experimental tools, including optical and magnetic tweezers, AFM, advanced microscopy, and microfluidics.

Soft & Active Matter

We investigate how activity, chirality, and geometric constraints drive flows, collective dynamics, and self-organized states.

Mechanobiology

Our work explores how cells sense, generate, and respond to mechanical forces, shaping processes like growth, migration, differentiation, and tissue organisation.

Biophysical Modelling

We develop models to understand cellular motion, cytoskeletal activity, and pattern formation in biological systems.
