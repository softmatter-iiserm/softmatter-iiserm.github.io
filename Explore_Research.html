Packing of a Helical Polymer in a Capsid
Soft & Active Matter

We study how torsional rigidity affects the packing and ejection of a helical semiflexible polymer inside a spherical capsid. A small amount of torsional stiffness minimizes packing time, while higher stiffness produces a non-monotonic slowdown–speedup behavior driven by competition between spooling tendencies and increased persistence length. Confinement is essential for this effect, and torsional rigidity strongly promotes spool-like structures. In contrast, ejection slows steadily with increasing torsional stiffness, showing how torsional elasticity governs both entry and exit dynamics in confined polymers.

From Genotype to Biomechanics: Force-Driven Folding in Cadherin-23 Variants
Mechanobiology

We dissect how oscillatory, sound-like mechanical forces drive stochastic folding–unfolding dynamics in tip-link proteins central to hearing. Using single-molecule force spectroscopy and in-silico modeling, we reveal mechanical adaptation that enables reliable mechano-transduction. We show that disease mutations impair this force adaptation, causing cooperative, failure-prone unfolding of tip-links. These mechanical vulnerabilities offer a molecular explanation for the severity of hearing loss and phenotypic variation across individuals.
